## Before adding a show to your list

Anime premieres usually move by season and by territory. A series can be announced for Crunchyroll in Spain, appear later in Mexico or still have no confirmed link in Argentina, Colombia or Chile.

That is why it helps to check three things first: release date, country and the exact platform. If a page does not show a provider yet, it does not always mean the show will be unavailable. It means confirmed availability is not present in the current data.

## How to use DondeAnime for premieres

Start with the next-week premiere page when you want something immediate. For a wider view, use the next-month list and then open the title page for your country. When availability exists, the page shows providers by territory and separates subscription, rent, buy, free and ad-supported options.

If a series is currently airing, check again during premiere week. Platforms update windows, languages and external links at different speeds.

## Signs of a useful title page

A strong page includes title, season, year, cover, synopsis and providers by country. If it also has a full release date, you can decide whether the anime fits your calendar or whether it is better to wait until more episodes are out.

DondeAnime does not host episodes. The goal is to send you to the official site or platform catalog when that data is available.

## Quick routine

- Check upcoming premieres to discover new series.
- Open the title page and switch country when sharing recommendations across Spain and Latin America.
- Confirm whether the provider is subscription, rent, buy, free or ad-supported.
- Save the page if availability is still missing and return later in the season.

That routine avoids starting a show without knowing where to watch it and reduces the usual jump between search engines, social feeds and incomplete catalogs.
